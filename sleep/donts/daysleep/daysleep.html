napping

we have all been guilty of this one. adults, students, babies, basically
everybody is an offender attempting to nap during the day to stay
awake during the night. turns out it's not exactly the best solution
out there...

prime suspects

  • lack of sleep
  • disrupted body clock
  • underlying health conditions

criminal effects

  • sleep inertia (disorientation after
    waking up)
  • sleep schedule disruption

verdict

  • keep naps short (10-30mins)
  • try to nap between 2-3pm
  • avoid napping at least a minimum
    of 4 hours before bedtime
